Q1:
I would use a hybrid approach that begins limited processing on partial transcripts while delaying critical decisions until the user finishes speaking. Partial transcripts arriving every 200ms can be useful for preparing context early, and  prefetching likely customer information. For example, if the partial transcript already contains keywords like “billing” or “internet not working”, the system can begin fetching customer records or ticket history in the background. This reduces wiating time once the full request is finalized.
However, querying the database aggressively on every partial transcript can create unnecessary load because speech recognition results often change as the user continues speaking. To avoid this, I would implement a strategy where the system waits for stable partial results or significant intent signals before triggering queries. Lightweight operations such as intent prediction or caching lookups can run early, while heavier queries ,such as analytics or escalation checks should wait for the final transcript.
The tradeoff is between responsiveness and efficiency. Early processing reduces response time but may waste resources if the transcript changes significantly. Waiting until completion ensures accuracy but increases waiting time. A hybrid strategy balances these concerns by performing selective early actions while delaying critical decisions until the final transcript is available.

Q2:
Automatically adding solutions with a CSAT score of 4 or higher to the knowledge base can help  support automation, but it also introduces risks over time. One potential issue is that the knowledge base may have incorrect or overly specific solutions. For example, a solution that worked for one customer’s router configuration might be added as a general recommendation even though it does not apply for everyone. Over six months this could lead to a knowledge base filled with inconsistent or misleading solutions. To mitigate this, I would introduce a review or validation layer where new entries must pass automated checks or  human review before being promoted to permanent knowledge articles.
A second issue is feedback bias. A customer may give a high CSAT score simply because the agent was polite or the interaction was quick, even if the technical resolution was incomplete. If such responses are automatically added to the knowledge base, the system may give an  ineffective  advice. To mitigate this, I would require multiple confirmations before adding a resolution. For example, a solution should only enter the knowledge base if it achieves high CSAT across multiple interactions or if its resolution rate exceeds a certain threshold. This ensures that the knowledge base evolves based on consistently successful solutions rather than isolated cases.

The most important improvement I would add is a comprehensive monitoring and feedback system for the AI support workflow. While the current system handles message processing, escalation decisions, and interaction storage, it lacks visibility into how well the AI performs in real-world conditions. Without monitoring, it becomes difficult to detect problems such as declining resolution rates, inaccurate responses, or excessive escalations.
I would implement a centralized metrics pipeline that tracks key performance indicators such as resolution rate by intent, average response time, escalation frequency, and customer satisfaction scores. These metrics would be visualized through dashboards and alerting systems so engineers and product teams can quickly identify abnormal trends.
Additionally, I would incorporate automated feedback loops where unresolved or escalated cases are analyzed to improve prompts, training data, or decision rules. For example, if a particular intent frequently leads to escalation, the system could flag it as a candidate for improved troubleshooting flows or updated knowledge base entries.
By introducing monitoring and feedback mechanisms, the system evolves from a static automation tool into a continuously improving support platform. This ensures that the AI becomes more accurate and effective over time while maintaining high service quality for customers.
